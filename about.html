We believe that users and companies mutually benefit from ethical practices, and that the tech industry is no exception.

    Our Approach

    Our approach to data ethics begins with the principle of informed consent. This generally means that a user must be informed as to the use of their data, and consent to it being used in that way. However, this isn't always as simple as a checkbox. Societal values and norms play a large role in what level of informed consent is required.


    Not all ethics challenges are data challenges. We, in some cases, also look for instances of racism or other unwanted discrimination within a business (don't worry, it won't be a white guy looking for racism). It's possible that data practices are perfectly ethical, but recruiting and marketing practices aren't, so we ensure we take a broad look at any company we examine.


    Ethics are almost universally subject to social norms. These, of course, vary by country. It's critical that we don't standardize to any one country--we consult with experts in any and all markets that may be required. This is also what allows us to support ethics across the globe, without being locked in to North America.
