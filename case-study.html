    Facial Recognition

    Recently, several companies have had issues with their facial recognition programs failing to properly recognize minority faces, as Buolamwini and Gebru of MIT and Microsoft discussed in their recent report. In most cases, the training data set's lack of diversity has been blamed for these results. This is not an acceptable answer and these issues are entirely avoidable.

    This is where Etiker comes in. K-Means clustering and random forests lend themselves well to analyzing potential racism or other unwanted discrimination within the algorithm. However, the growth of deep learning has by and large not been matched with analysis tools. This is understandable due to the complexity of neural networks, however, this cannot and should not serve as an excuse for unethical practices.
